Welcome to Nehemia Ministry Kenya

Transforming lives through the power of God's love since 2006. Founded by the visionary leadership of Bishop David Walukhu and Reverend Selina Walukhu in the blessed grounds of Mukhonje, Kakamega County, we stand as a beacon of hope, healing, and spiritual transformation in Western Kenya and beyond.

Our ministry encompasses comprehensive spiritual care, community development, healthcare through our Kingdom Faith-Based Hospital, educational excellence via Matco Training Institute, and compassionate care for orphans through New Promise Home. We are more than a church—we are a family united in Christ's mission to heal, teach, and love unconditionally.

Our Visionary Founders

Bishop David Walukhu

Senior Pastor & Founder

Bishop David Walukhu is a man of extraordinary vision and unwavering faith, called by God to establish Nehemia Ministry Kenya in 2006. With over two decades of ministerial experience, he has been instrumental in transforming countless lives through his powerful preaching, prophetic ministry, and compassionate leadership.

Born and raised in Western Kenya, Bishop David experienced a dramatic encounter with Christ in his early twenties that completely revolutionized his life. From that moment, he dedicated himself to full-time ministry, studying theology extensively and serving in various capacities before receiving the divine mandate to establish Nehemia Ministry.

His ministry is characterized by signs, wonders, and miracles, with thousands testifying to physical healings, spiritual breakthroughs, and life transformations under his pastoral care. Bishop David's heart for the community led to the establishment of Kingdom Faith-Based Hospital, providing healthcare to the underserved, and Matco Training Institute, equipping young people with practical skills for economic empowerment.

Beyond preaching, Bishop David is an accomplished author, conference speaker, and mentor to many pastors across East Africa. His teachings on faith, prosperity, and divine healing have reached millions through radio, television, and digital platforms.

Reverend Selina Walukhu

Co-Founder & Women's Ministry Leader

Reverend Selina Walukhu stands as a pillar of strength and wisdom in Nehemia Ministry Kenya. Her partnership with Bishop David in ministry exemplifies the biblical model of unity in purpose and calling. She brings unique gifts of counseling, women's ministry, and pastoral care that have touched thousands of lives.

With a special calling to minister to women, children, and families, Reverend Selina has developed comprehensive programs addressing domestic challenges, women's empowerment, child care, and family restoration. Her gentle yet powerful ministry style has earned her recognition as one of Kenya's most effective women ministers.

She oversees the New Promise Home for orphaned and vulnerable children, ensuring they receive not just physical care but spiritual nurturing and educational opportunities. Under her leadership, dozens of children have been raised to become productive members of society, with many now serving in various ministry capacities.

Reverend Selina's expertise in Christian counseling and family therapy has helped restore countless marriages and families. She conducts regular seminars on marriage, parenting, and women's spiritual development, drawing participants from across the region.

Her heart for missions has led to the establishment of women's ministry branches in neighboring countries, creating a network of empowered women leaders across East Africa.

About Nehemia Ministry Kenya

A Legacy of Faith, Hope, and Transformation Since 2006

Nehemia Ministry Kenya stands as a testament to God's faithfulness and the power of vision combined with unwavering dedication. What began as a small fellowship in Mukhonje, Kakamega County, has grown into a multi-faceted ministry touching lives across Kenya and East Africa. Our name "Nehemia" draws inspiration from the biblical Nehemiah, the rebuilder of Jerusalem's walls, symbolizing our calling to rebuild broken lives, communities, and dreams.

Our ministry philosophy centers on holistic transformation—addressing not just spiritual needs but also physical, emotional, educational, and socio-economic challenges. We believe that true Christianity manifests in practical love, demonstrated through action and service to humanity. This conviction has led us to establish comprehensive programs that serve the whole person.

From our humble beginnings with just a handful of believers, we have grown to serve over 5,000 regular congregants across multiple locations, with satellite churches in Uganda, Tanzania, and Southern Sudan. Our influence extends far beyond church walls through our healthcare, education, and social programs that benefit tens of thousands annually.

Our theological foundation rests firmly on biblical orthodoxy while embracing the fullness of the Gospel—salvation, healing, deliverance, and prosperity as God's will for His people. We practice water baptism by immersion, believe in the baptism of the Holy Spirit with evidence of speaking in tongues, and maintain that divine healing is available to all believers today.

The ministry operates under a comprehensive vision encompassing evangelism, discipleship, church planting, community development, healthcare delivery, education, and humanitarian aid. We have trained and released over 200 ministers who now serve in various capacities across East Africa, multiplying our impact exponentially.

Our Life-Changing Ministries

Transforming Communities Through Comprehensive Service

Kingdom Faith-Based Hospital

Mukhonje, Kakamega County

Our state-of-the-art medical facility serves as a beacon of hope and healing in Western Kenya. Established with the vision of providing affordable, quality healthcare while ministering to the spiritual needs of patients, Kingdom Faith-Based Hospital has treated over 50,000 patients since its inception.

The hospital features modern diagnostic equipment, a well-equipped laboratory, pharmacy, maternity ward, pediatric unit, and surgical theater. Our medical team comprises qualified doctors, nurses, and support staff who combine professional excellence with compassionate Christian service.

Special programs include free medical camps in rural areas, maternal health initiatives, HIV/AIDS counseling and treatment, nutrition programs for malnourished children, and health education campaigns. We've witnessed countless miraculous healings alongside medical interventions, demonstrating God's power in healthcare.

Matco Training Institute

Malaha, Technical & Vocational Education

Matco Training Institute stands as a premier technical and vocational education center, equipping young people with marketable skills for economic empowerment. Our comprehensive curriculum combines technical training with Christian values, producing graduates who are not only skilled but also morally upright.

We offer courses in automotive mechanics, electrical installation, plumbing, computer studies, tailoring and fashion design, catering and hospitality, agriculture and agribusiness, and entrepreneurship development. Our workshops are equipped with modern tools and equipment for hands-on learning.

Over 2,000 students have graduated from our programs, with 85% securing employment or starting successful businesses within six months of graduation. Our job placement program connects graduates with employers, while our microfinance initiative provides startup capital for entrepreneurial ventures.

Explore Courses

New Promise Home

Lwandeti, Orphanage & Child Care

New Promise Home represents our commitment to caring for orphaned, abandoned, and vulnerable children. This comprehensive childcare facility provides a loving, nurturing environment where children receive not only basic needs but also spiritual guidance, education, and preparation for successful adult life.

Currently home to 120 children aged 3-18 years, the facility features modern dormitories, a dining hall, library, computer lab, playground, and chapel. Our dedicated staff includes trained caregivers, teachers, counselors, and social workers who ensure each child receives individualized attention and care.

Beyond basic care, we provide formal education through partnerships with local schools, vocational training for older children, counseling and therapy services, spiritual nurturing and discipleship, and family reintegration programs where possible. Many of our graduates have gone on to universities and successful careers, with several now serving in ministry.
